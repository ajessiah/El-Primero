Welcome to
El Primero

While today’s yachts impress with advanced engineering and contemporary design, some of the most remarkable feats of craftsmanship were achieved long before the modern era. Among these enduring icons stands El Primero, a vessel whose legacy of innovation and elegance continues to inspire more than a century after her launch.

Built in 1893 and aptly named “The First,” El Primero was the very first steam yacht constructed on the West Coast of America. Her design drew inspiration from Native American ocean canoes, with a Clipper bow and hull that mirrored the angles of early seafaring vessels. The result was a yacht as graceful under sail as under steam—originally outfitted with emergency sails to ensure reliability in any condition.

Stretching over 120 feet in length, El Primero was a marvel of her time. Her teak and mahogany interiors featured hand-carved cabinetry, while twin 225-horsepower triple-expansion engines powered a cruising range of over 5,000 miles. Designed to carry up to 22 guests with a full crew, she drew just four feet at the bow and five at the stern, making her equally suited to open-water voyages and shallow coastal exploration.

Renowned as the most luxurious yacht on the West Coast through the early and mid-1900s, El Primero hosted four U.S. presidents—Taft, Harding, Hoover, and Roosevelt—and outlasted nearly all of her contemporaries. By the 1950s, she remained one of only two operational steam yachts on Puget Sound, a living link to a bygone maritime age. Today, our mission is to restore El Primero to her rightful place in history—preserving her timeless craftsmanship while giving new generations an authentic glimpse into the vanished elegance of America’s nautical past.
